Thor Overview

Thor (2011) is the fourth film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) and introduces audiences to the Norse God of Thunder, Thor, played by Chris Hemsworth. Directed by Kenneth Branagh, the film follows Thor as he is banished from Asgard and sent to Earth to live as a mortal. Thor must prove his worthiness as a hero, and in the process, he faces the villainous Loki, his adopted brother, and the threat of the destroyer. This film sets up Thor's future in the MCU and his eventual leadership in the Avengers.

  • Cast: Chris Hemsworth, Natalie Portman, Tom Hiddleston, Anthony Hopkins, Clark Gregg
  • Genre: Action, Adventure, Fantasy

About the Film

Thor is the son of Odin, the King of Asgard, but his reckless behavior leads to his banishment to Earth, where he is forced to live among mortals. On Earth, he teams up with scientist Jane Foster (Natalie Portman) and faces off against his adopted brother, Loki (Tom Hiddleston), who seeks to rule Asgard and destroy Earth. As Thor learns the importance of humility and sacrifice, he becomes a true hero and gains the respect of both mortals and gods.
