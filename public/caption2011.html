Captain America: The First Avenger Overview

Captain America: The First Avenger (2011) introduces Steve Rogers (played by Chris Evans), a frail young man from Brooklyn who dreams of serving in the U.S. military during World War II. After being rejected multiple times due to his health, he becomes the subject of a secret military experiment that transforms him into the super-soldier known as Captain America. As Captain America, he faces the Red Skull, the leader of Hydra, who seeks to harness the power of the Tesseract to conquer the world. Directed by Joe Johnston, this film sets the stage for Captain America's future role in the Avengers.

  • Cast: Chris Evans, Hayley Atwell, Tommy Lee Jones, Hugo Weaving, Sebastian Stan

About the Film

During World War II, Steve Rogers, a young and weak man from Brooklyn, gets rejected from military service multiple times. However, he is chosen for a secret government program where he is injected with the Super Soldier Serum, transforming him into the physically enhanced Captain America. He becomes a symbol of hope, fighting against the villainous Red Skull (Hugo Weaving), who controls Hydra and seeks to use the Tesseract's power to dominate the world. As Captain America, Rogers faces the moral challenges of leadership, duty, and sacrifice as he fights to protect the world from the forces of evil.
